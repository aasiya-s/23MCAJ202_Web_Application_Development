Kerala University

Introduction

The University of Kerala, originally established as the University of Travancore in 1937, stands as one of India's premier institutions of higher education. Nestled in the capital city of Thiruvananthapuram, the university has evolved over the decades to become a hub of academic excellence and research innovation, serving thousands of students across a diverse range of disciplines.

The university's inception marked a significant milestone in the history of education in Kerala. Founded by the Maharaja of Travancore, Sri Chithira Thirunal Balarama Varma, the institution aimed to provide advanced education and promote scholarly pursuits. In 1957, the University of Travancore was reconstituted as the University of Kerala, following the reorganization of Indian states.
